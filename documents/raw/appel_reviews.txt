Andrew Appel's teaching style is rigorous and detail-oriented. Students describe his lectures as thorough and sometimes dense, with a strong focus on correctness and systems-level reasoning. The course is known for its heavy workload, and many reviewers say it requires a lot of time outside class.

Homework and exams are difficult but instructive. Several reviewers mentioned that the assignments are challenging and that grading is strict but fair. The exams test understanding of the concepts rather than rote memorization.

Students who take Appel's classes should expect to read carefully, work consistently, and ask questions early. The course is often described as demanding, but the professors are seen as knowledgeable and helpful when students are prepared.

Several reviewers mentioned projects that required debugging real systems code, which they found both exciting and time-consuming. The class also includes frequent quizzes and short in-class assessments, so staying current with the material is important.

The final exam was described as fair but comprehensive, testing a broad range of topics from lectures and homework. Many students advised future takers to use previous assignments as study guides and to focus on understanding fundamentals rather than memorizing syntax.
