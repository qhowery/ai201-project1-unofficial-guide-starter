Kevin Wayne's classes are organized and lecture-heavy. Reviewers consistently describe his lectures as clear but fast, with a strong emphasis on proofs and algorithmic thinking. He communicates the big ideas well, but students should expect to review proofs multiple times outside of class.

The homework sets are considered difficult and time-consuming. Students say that the problem sets are proof-oriented and require careful writing. Some reviewers also note that the exams are challenging and require a strong understanding of the material rather than simple computation.

For students who enjoy theory, Wayne's course is rewarding, but those who are weaker in proofs may find the workload steep. Office hours were described as helpful, though they can fill up quickly before exams.

Some reviewers said the textbook is useful but not sufficient by itself; they needed lecture notes and external practice problems to fully understand the material. Grades were said to be reflective of effort, and missing problem set deadlines could significantly lower a student's grade.

In addition, several students noted that collaboration was encouraged on homework when it was done properly, but clarity around what counts as collaboration could have been better.
