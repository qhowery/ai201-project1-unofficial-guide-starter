Brian Kernighan is an excellent lecturer, but his class is intense. His lectures move quickly and expect students to follow the logic without expecting too much hand-holding. Many students said his teaching was clear if you read the material before class, but the pace can be overwhelming if you rely only on lecture notes.

Homework in his course is challenging and often graded strictly. Several reviewers mentioned that homework problems are conceptually deep and not always directly covered in lecture, so consistent practice is important. Exams are described as technically demanding and focused on problem-solving rather than memorization.

Overall, students recommend attending every lecture and starting assignments early. One reviewer said the course is rewarding for students who enjoy rigorous programming and algorithms, but it can feel like a lot of work if you are not prepared for the pace.

The professor is also praised for being approachable after class, and many students appreciated having a clear set of expectations for the final project. Some criticisms centered on the lack of review sessions, with students wishing there were more sample problems available before exams.

A few reviewers explicitly warned future students not to underestimate the course. They recommended forming study groups and reviewing lecture material daily to stay on top of the content.
